# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Focus Todo is for individual macOS users who want a small, always-nearby focus tool rather than a full project-management app. They are likely switching between writing, studying, coding, browsing, and messaging, and they need a compact way to choose the next task, run a Pomodoro session, keep notes, and block distracting sites without opening a large window.

The main context is repeated, low-friction use from the menu bar or floating panels. The interface should stay quiet enough to sit beside real work, but clear enough that the user can read task state, timer state, and blocking state at a glance.

## Product Purpose

Focus Todo combines a daily todo widget, Pomodoro timer, memo panel, and website blocker into one lightweight macOS utility. Success means the user can decide what to work on, start a focus session, capture notes, and reduce browser distractions in a few seconds.

The product should make focus feel manageable and calm. It should not try to become a full planning suite, analytics dashboard, habit tracker, or social productivity system.

## Brand Personality

Calm, simple, focused.

The product voice is brief and practical. Korean UI copy should feel natural and direct, with labels that fit compact panels and avoid motivational clutter. The emotional target is a tidy desk: enough warmth to feel personal, enough restraint to keep attention on the task.

## Anti-references

Avoid dense enterprise dashboards, productivity apps that turn every action into a metric, and SaaS-style layouts full of repeated cards. Avoid loud gradients, gamified reward systems, aggressive badges, neon focus themes, and decorative glass effects.

The app should not look like a marketing site, a task database, a kanban board, or a game overlay. It should also avoid mimicking Notion too literally: the current influence is useful for softness and clarity, but the product needs its own compact macOS rhythm.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the next action close. The primary task, timer action, memo action, and blocker management should stay reachable without adding navigation depth.
2. Protect attention. Use restrained color, short labels, and predictable layout so the tool does not compete with the user's actual work.
3. Fit the menu-bar scale. Every component must work in narrow floating panels, with stable dimensions and no layout jumps when rows hover, timers tick, or Korean text changes length.
4. Show state quietly. Active, done, blocked, and paused states should be visible through small but reliable signals, not oversized banners or celebration patterns.
5. Prefer inline flow over interruption. Add, edit, manage, and recover actions should happen in-place when possible, with modals or separate panels reserved for genuinely larger tasks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls where platform rendering allows. Do not rely on color alone for task completion, active timer state, or blocking status. Keep hit targets reasonable for a small desktop utility, preserve keyboard-oriented workflows, and ensure text remains readable when macOS accessibility font or contrast settings are enabled.

Motion should be subtle, brief, and nonessential. Avoid bounce, elastic motion, or attention-seeking animation; provide stable states for users who prefer reduced motion.
